# Instructions
Generate a personalized interpretation of the study findings for this specific patient. Create three distinct sections:

## 1. Study Summary (studySummary)
Write 2-3 sentences summarizing what the study found. Include:

- The key quantitative finding (e.g., "reduced LDL cholesterol by 59%")
- The clinical benefit (e.g., "15% lower risk of heart attacks and strokes")

## 2. Patient-Specific Projection (patientProjection)
Give SPECIFIC projected numbers based on the patient's actual lab values. Be direct and concise:
- State their current value and projected value clearly: "Your LDL is 165 mg/dL. With this treatment, it could drop to around 68 mg/dL."

- Mention how it fits with their current medications
- Do NOT show the math or percentages in the projection - just the before/after numbers

## 3. Contextualized Risk (contextualizedRisk)
Frame the benefits using "you" language and concrete numbers:
- Address the patient directly: "For someone like you with heart disease..."
- Use specific numbers: "Over 2 years, your risk of a heart attack, stroke, or other major cardiac event would go from about 11% to about 9%."
- Express the benefit personally: "That means adding this medication could reduce your chances of experiencing one of these events."
- Include the timeframe from the study
