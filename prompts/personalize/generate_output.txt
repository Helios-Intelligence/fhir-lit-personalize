# Instructions
Generate a personalized interpretation of the study findings for this specific patient. Create three distinct sections:

## 1. Study Summary (studySummary)
Write 2-3 sentences summarizing what the study found. Include:
- The medication name and what it does
- The key quantitative finding (e.g., "reduced LDL cholesterol by 59%")
- The clinical benefit (e.g., "15% lower risk of heart attacks and strokes")

## 2. Patient-Specific Projection (patientProjection)
Give SPECIFIC projected numbers based on the patient's actual lab values. Be direct and concise:
- ALWAYS state their current value and projected value with numbers:
  - For LDL (percent reduction): "Your LDL is 165 mg/dL. With this treatment, it could drop to around 68 mg/dL."
  - For HbA1c (absolute change): "Your HbA1c is 8.2%. This treatment could lower it to around 7.8%."
  - For blood pressure: "Your systolic BP is 145 mmHg. This could bring it down to around 125 mmHg."
- Include the dosing: route, dose, and frequency (e.g., "daily injection of 1.8 mg", "10 mg tablet once daily")
- If different doses have different effects, present both options with their respective outcomes
- Mention how it fits with their current medications
- Do NOT show the math or percentages - just the before/after numbers
- If no relevant lab value exists, use the study's baseline to estimate: "Based on patients like you in the study..."

## 3. Contextualized Risk (contextualizedRisk)
Frame the benefits using "you" language and CONCRETE NUMBERS - this is critical:
- Address the patient directly: "For someone like you with [their conditions]..."
- ALWAYS calculate and state specific event rates using the study's baseline rate and hazard ratio:
  - If baseline rate is 13.4% and HR is 0.87: "Over 3.8 years, your risk of a major cardiovascular event would go from about 13% to about 11%."
  - If baseline rate is 11.3% and HR is 0.85: "Over 2.2 years, your risk would go from about 11% to about 9%."
- Express the absolute benefit: "That's roughly 2 fewer events per 100 people treated."
- Include the study timeframe
- NEVER say "your risk could be reduced" without giving the actual numbers

## 4. Suggested Next Step (suggestedAction) - REQUIRED
You MUST provide ONE specific, actionable step the patient can take. Be concrete and direct:
- If biomarker data is missing: "Ask your doctor to order a lipid panel to check your LDL cholesterol levels."
- If treatment is relevant: "Schedule an appointment with your cardiologist to discuss whether this medication might be right for you."
- If lifestyle is relevant: "Consider scheduling a consultation with a registered dietitian to optimize your diet for heart health."
- For monitoring: "Request your next HbA1c test to track your progress."
- Keep it to ONE action, not a list
- Make it specific to what was learned from this study
- Examples:
  - "Book an appointment with your doctor to discuss adding evolocumab to your current statin therapy."
  - "Ask your doctor about getting a lipid panel to see if your LDL is at goal."
  - "Schedule a follow-up to discuss whether an SGLT2 inhibitor could benefit your kidney function."
