# Instructions
Generate a personalized interpretation of the study findings for this specific patient. Create three distinct sections:

## 1. Study Summary (studySummary)
Write 2-3 sentences summarizing what the study found. Include:
- The intervention and dosing regimen (e.g., "evolocumab 140 mg injected every 2 weeks or 420 mg monthly")
- The key quantitative finding (e.g., "reduced LDL cholesterol by 59%")
- The clinical outcome (e.g., "15% lower risk of heart attacks and strokes")

## 2. Patient-Specific Projection (patientProjection)
Calculate and show SPECIFIC numbers based on the patient's actual lab values:
- If LDL is available and the study shows % reduction, CALCULATE the projected LDL:
  Example: "Your LDL is currently 165 mg/dL. Based on this study's 59% reduction, adding evolocumab could potentially lower your LDL to around 68 mg/dL."
- Include the exact dosing options from the intervention
- Mention how this would work with their current medications
- Show the math clearly so patients understand the projection

## 3. Contextualized Risk (contextualizedRisk)
Frame the benefits using concrete "N out of 100" language:
- Use the hazard ratio and baseline event rate to calculate absolute risk reduction
- Example: "Over 2.2 years in this study, about 11 out of 100 patients on standard treatment had a cardiovascular event. With evolocumab added, only about 10 out of 100 did - meaning roughly 1-2 fewer events per 100 patients treated."
- Include the Number Needed to Treat (NNT) if available

# Tone Guidelines
- Conversational and empowering, not alarming
- Use "you" and "your" to personalize
- Include appropriate caveats ("Based on this study...", "Individual results may vary...")
- Be specific with numbers - patients want to know actual projections
- Be honest about limitations

# Calculation Guidelines
- For LDL reduction: new_LDL = current_LDL × (1 - percent_reduction)

- Always show the patient's starting value and the projected value
- Round to sensible precision (whole numbers for LDL, one decimal for percentages)
